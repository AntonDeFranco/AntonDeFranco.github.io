Introduction¶

During the Covid-19 pandemic, the U.S. Government under the CARES act gave small businesses the opportunity to apply and receive forgivable PPP loans. The data for who recieved a PPP loan, and for how much, is publicly available online at the SBA.gov website. I would consider this data to be extremely reliable and overall very accurate, as it comes directly from the government. The overall PPP loan information is split into several subsets of data, with one file consisting of loans data over \$150k USD and 12 other files consisting of all other loans under $150k USD. The dataset contains many variables. A comprehensive list of all variables is found in this data dictionary. Loan data is segmented somewhat by state, and is not input at random.

I am working with this data primarily because of it's relevancy. The Covid-19 pandemic and general shutdown of the U.S. happened extremely recently, and the PPP loans were the result of a hasty government response to help struggling businesses during the pandemic. The amount of data pertaining to the PPP loans is quite extensive, and I feel as if there are important questions to be answered be poking and proding this data.

Second Dataset¶

I will also bring in another dataset into this analysis. I would like to explore the relationship between poverty in the United States, and and how that may or may not affect the PPP Loans amounts taken. To faciliate this, I went to the USDA website and found a dataset of poverty by state. This was further serparated into children (0-17 years old) in poverty and all people in poverty. I am not sure how relevant this will be, but I think it might provide some interesting insight.

Project Goals¶

I will be investigating how several variables such as Gender, Veteran Status, and Ethnicity are correlated with the PPP loan amount and how much of the loan will be forgiven. This PPP data is publicly available at this government website. By analyzing this data, I would like to answer questions such as:

  1. Did one specific ethnicity or race borrow more money compared to others?
  2. Do states with higher poverty rates have more PPP loans on file?
  3. Did people who identify as a veteran borrow more or less on average than those who did not identify as veteran?
  4. Could I identify other relevant features that might explain a difference in loan amounts with men compared to women?
  5. Were the loan amounts equitable?

For this project, I define equitable as the PPP loan amount for women and men being the same.

I was having issues with the built in Pandas get_dummies function. It did not run in any reasonable amount of tieme, so I decided to do my own version of dummy encoding for the regions. This is what my code does when run:

  1. Create a list of each state in each region.
  2. Add states to the correct region list.
  3. Create four columns (or bins) each named one of the regions in the PPP Dataframe.
  4. Add a 1 to the column if the borrower's state is in the correct bin and 0 for the rest.

This solved my issue with the get_dummies function, and allows me to incorporate the regions into my model.
